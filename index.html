Helping Riders Stay Safe

Problem Statemnt

Majority of products for motorcyclists are tailored for convenience rather than safety. Typically riders must manually check their blindspots via side-mirrors and shoulder checks. This becomes a hazard for riders as they become distracted when doing so. After vehicle accidents, there is no efficient way contacting emergency services in products on the market. Our solution will address these issues by notifying riders of necessary information while on the road and providing post-crash safety measures to better their safety.

Intro to SafeX

Project Goal

There are alot of safety features available for cars that are not accessible to motorcycles. Additionally, there are also missing features that should be available to motorcyclists. We hope to bring some of those safety features to motorcycles in order to ensure the safety of riders.

To create a two part device that is attachable to a motorcycle and helmet. The system should notify riders of oncoming vehicles within their blindspots to increase rider safety, and alert emergency services if the rider gets into a crash.

Documentation: Design Research

tl;dr: For our initial project idea we wanted to implement a heads up display on the helmet that would provide the rider with important information. The display would allow the rider to see their blindspots similar to rear view cameras on most modern vehicles. It would also be useful to provide additional information like incoming phone notifications, live navigation, etc. This would mitigate riders from having to consistently check their dashboard which can become a hazard. As such it must be crucial that this display doesn't obstruct the rider's view. There are some market motorcycle helmets that have implemented a heads up display.

Documentation: Blindspot Detection Field Research

tl;dr: To understand how we should implement the user interface for the blindspot detection system, we conducted a survey where participants (consisting of peers, acquaintances and online forums) provided feedback on their opinions on where they would like to recieve blindspot notification; On their helmet or dash. From this we were able to conclude that the majority of our participants had concerns about a live camera feed of thier blindspot area because they feared that it would be too distracting.
Instead the majority prefered an LED system on their helmet that notified them of objects in the blindspot and their location (left or right side) with respects to the rider.

Documentation: Fall Detection Research

tl;dr: One of our other main subsystems for our project was fall detection since motorcycle accidents are common and more severe. Our idea was to implemnt a way for the system to detect accidents using an accelerometer and gyroscope so that a fall can be accurately detected. The accelerometer could also be further improved upon (post SDP) to recieve data from the accident (information like speed, position, decleration, etc.) so that it can be used during post accident investigation.
